Phoebe Joy Lim

Aesop

Role

ideation, copywriting, graphic design, video editing, illustration

Team

Nikita Tauro, Jenny Ngyuyen, Jessica Yuki Huang, Christy Lum

Challenge

Aesop, a high-end skincare brand, hosts local arts events. But because they do not believe in conventional ads, they have limited attendance. How might we increase the reach of these small art communities without compromising Aesop's brand?

Process

Defining Aesop's audience

We first identified out persona. To inform our business problem, we conducted interviews with Aesop customers and store consultants. These first-hand encounters informed who we are designing for. We crafted a persona based on these findings.

Poetry as inspiration

“I like ideas and products that reveal themselves slowly – more whisper than scream, something that becomes part of our own personal universe.”
— Dennis Paphitis, Aesop Founder

We wanted to incorporate this idea of something that "reveals itself slowly", that "whispers" and becomes a "part of our own personal universe." Poetry is a big part of Aesop as a brand, so creating a poetic and artistic end product was a must. We then conducted a sprint in order to quickly generate ideas in the span of 3 days.

Outcome

A browser extension paired with a microsite, using artwork as a digital invitation to get art enthusiasts into Aesop stores.

Takeaways

  1. Don't get too lost in what the brand is. Of course the design should feel like Aesop, but don't let it block you from ideating early on.

  2. Think about technologies that are under-utilized, and how they can be better implemented and adapted (i.e. browser extensions)
