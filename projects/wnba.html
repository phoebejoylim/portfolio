Phoebe Joy Lim

WNBA

Role

ideation, copywriting, graphic design, illustration, video production

Team

Nikita Tauro, Jenny Ngyuyen, Jessica Yuki Huang, Christy Lum

Challenge

Viewership of WNBA games have been declining since 2010, but saw a small amount of success this year with new partnerships and marketing. How can we ensure that these efforts lead to long-lasting fans?

Process

Identifying the business problem

First task was to figure out WNBA's business problem. Based off our research, we knew there was a decline in viewership since 2010. There are many contributing factors to the declining revenue, but the goal remained the same: they needed to grow new fans.

Defining our audience focus

We needed a clear audience in order to inform our design. First, we thought focusing on bringing college basketball fans into the fold. However, after interviewing basketball fans, and further research, we realized that college fans already have a bias against the WNBA.

With this in mind, we switched to focusing on families, an already existing demographic for the WNBA. We decided that getting kids engaged was the best possible route for building brand loyalty early on. We created two personas, one for the child, and a secondary one for the parent.

Mapping the journey

We created a journey map of the game experience from the perspective of our personas, and decided on the 'enter' and 'extend' touchpoints for our design, since we didn't want to intrude on the actual game experience.

Testing key users

We then created a prototype, using all our collective insights, and tested it with 7 kids and 4 parents. The key findings were:

  1. Kids love graphic novels

  2. Don’t include in-app purchases

  3. Make social issues a subtle part of the interface.

From there, we refined our prototype to focus on engaging kids, telling stories, and promoting collectibles to encourage continued use.

Outcome

Grow young teen athletes who are casual fans into involved, loyal fans by showing personable stories of professional players in a fun mobile app.

Takeaways

  1. What it means to be truly empathetic, and understand your audience, their needs and limitations.

  2. Never assume what someone is like. Make a preliminary persona, then do research and user testing to validate hypothesis.

  3. Your audience might not be who you first expect. Outline the possible stakeholders first, and determine which ones will help the design goal and benefit from the intervention.

  4. I have a new appreciation for women’s basketball. Getting to know the WNBA players and their stories was really inspiring!
